title: "How to Set Up Passkey Sign-In for Your SPCX Account"

description: "Register a biometric or device-PIN passkey to sign in to SPCX Market faster and more securely, without waiting for an email sign-in link."
---

A passkey lets you sign in to KitaFund SPCX Market using your device's built-in authentication — Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint, or a device PIN — instead of receiving an email sign-in link. Because passkeys are tied to your physical device and use on-device cryptography, they are resistant to phishing and more secure than email-based sign-in. Once registered, signing in takes just a single biometric confirmation.

## Set Up Your Passkey

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings">
    Open the sidebar by tapping the menu icon in the top-left corner of any page, then tap **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find 'Sign In with Passkey'">
    In Settings, locate the **Passkey** row under the Account section and tap it to begin registration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register your passkey">
    Follow your device's prompts to create the passkey. Depending on your device, you will authenticate with **Face ID**, **Touch ID**, or your **device PIN**. Your device stores the passkey securely — it never leaves your device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use your passkey to sign in">
    On your next visit to SPCX Market, choose **Sign In with Passkey** on the sign-in screen instead of entering your email. Your device will prompt you for biometric confirmation and you will be signed in immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Requirements

Your device must support the passkey standard to use this feature. The following environments are compatible:

- **iOS** 16 or later (Face ID or Touch ID)
- **Android** 9 or later with a screen lock configured
- **Desktop browsers** — Chrome, Safari, or Edge on macOS or Windows with a platform authenticator enabled

If your device does not meet these requirements, passkey registration will not be available and you can continue signing in with your email link.

<Tip>
  Passkeys are more secure than email sign-in links because they cannot be intercepted, forwarded, or phished. They are also faster — a single biometric tap replaces the full email round-trip.
</Tip>

<Note>
  If your passkey device is unavailable — for example, you are signing in on a new device — you can always fall back to **Sign In with Email** on the sign-in screen. Your passkey remains registered for when you return to your usual device.
</Note>
